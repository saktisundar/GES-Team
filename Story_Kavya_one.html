A GLIMPSE OF PROFESSOR MOUSE’S CLASS

Name : Kavya D

Class : VI

Day : Tuesday

Date : 4.5.2021

Many light years away from earth, on planet Pretzel, Professor Mouse was taking class. Yet his name described the opposite of him.
“He should have been called Professor Dragolion” (the most dangerous creature on Pretzel) all the kids say. Professor Mouse had always thought that children were bonk heads and it was up to him to make sure they come out right.
Professor Mouse was in a particularly fierce temper today; tomorrow there was a math test and Principle Gloopna was depending on him to make the class pass.
“SETTLE DOWN CLASS” he bellowed. The once noisy class became quiet. Yet Professor Mouse wasn’t satisfied at all (he never was).
“Marna!!! What did I say about writing your homework in glitter pens!?
Marna was red with embarrassment.
“I’m sorry Professor “she said in a small voice. I lost my normal pens so I thou-
“That’s no excuse” Professor Mouse growled. “SIMPSON!! Is that hair dye?!?! And you BLOOMER! Tuck IN your shirt! OLGA! This is a CLASSROOM not GROWLYWOOD (Pretzel’s version of Hollywood) so take OFF those ridiculous shoes! MORGLOP!?!?!? IS THAT A TATTOO!!?
After making several complaints and adjustment, Professor Mouse finally revised various math formulas that were bound to show up in the test. He found that none of the children were prepared. This enraged him more for the rest of the class, which finished with him screeching: “I EXPECT ALL YOU CHILDREN TO STUDY AND HAVE THE FORMULAS ON YOUR FINGERTIPS BY TOMORROW!!!!!!!!! LITERALLY!!!! With this he slammed the door.
***********************
The next day Professor Mouse walked into his class rather nervous. To his surprise all his children were as silent as mice and none of them were wearing tattoos or high heels or hair dye, all of them had tucked in their shirts and no one had written their homework in glitter pens (he hoped).
He smiled at them briefly and began handing out test papers when he noticed something wrong. All his pupils had scrawled something on their fingers! On their fingertips more specifically.
“Marna will you kindly explain what you and your classmates have done to your fingers”?
“We’ve been doing what you’ve said Professor “said Marna earnestly. “We’re having the formulas on our fingertips! Just like you said.”
Professor Mouse slapped his forehead with his hand. He had no idea what to do- to hysterically burst into fits of laughs or to run frantically run to the principle for help. Professor Mouse might have not known what to do, but he now did know that children were smart in their own way.
THE END
